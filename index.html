Snapchat Planets: Meaning, Order, Ranking and Full Guide

The keyword Snapchat Planets is searched by users who want to understand why planets appear on a friend’s profile, what each planet means, and how Snapchat decides friendship positions. Many Snapchat users see planet names like Mercury, Venus, Earth, or Neptune and feel confused because the feature looks fun but does not explain everything at first glance.

Snapchat Planets are part of Snapchat’s Friend Solar System feature for Snapchat+ users. In this system, your friend is shown like the Sun, and your position in their friendship circle appears as a planet. Snapchat says that when a Snapchat+ subscriber sees a “Best Friends” badge with a gold ring on someone’s Friendship Profile, tapping that badge can show which planet they are in that friend’s Solar System. Each planet represents a different position in the person’s Best Friends list.

Meaning

What Are Snapchat Planets?

Snapchat Planets are a visual ranking system used to show how close you are to a friend based on Snapchat interaction. Instead of showing only a number, Snapchat uses the solar system idea. The closer the planet is to the Sun, the higher the friendship rank.

For example, Mercury is closest to the Sun, so it represents the number one position. Neptune is farthest away, so it represents the eighth position. This makes the feature easy to understand once you know the order.

The feature is connected with Snapchat+, which is Snapchat’s paid subscription offering premium, exclusive, experimental, and pre-release features. Because of this, not every normal Snapchat user will see the full Friend Solar System feature.

How It Works

How Snapchat Planets Work

Snapchat Planets work by showing your position in someone else’s Best Friends list. The important point is that you are not always looking at your own ranking. When you open a friend’s profile and tap the badge, the planet shows where you stand in their Snapchat Solar System.

If you appear as Mercury, it means you are very close to that person on Snapchat. If you appear as Neptune, it means you are still in their top eight but at the eighth position. This ranking is based on Snapchat activity such as snapping and chatting, but Snapchat does not publicly explain the full exact formula.

This is why users should not treat Snapchat Planets as a perfect measurement of real friendship. It is mainly an app-based interaction feature, not a complete judgment of personal relationships.

Planet Meanings

Meaning of Each Snapchat Planet

1

Mercury on Snapchat

Mercury is the first planet and the closest planet to the Sun. On Snapchat, Mercury means you are in the first position in your friend’s Solar System. This usually suggests that you and that friend interact very often through Snaps or Chats.

2

Venus on Snapchat

Venus is the second planet. If you see Venus, it means you are the second closest friend in that person’s Snapchat ranking. This is still a strong position and shows regular interaction.

3

Earth on Snapchat

Earth represents the third position. It means you are among the top friends but not at the first or second spot. Many users see Earth as a balanced and strong friendship position.

4

Mars on Snapchat

Mars is the fourth planet. If you appear as Mars, it means your Snapchat connection is active, but three other friends are ranked higher in that person’s Best Friends list.

5

Jupiter on Snapchat

Jupiter is the fifth planet. This position means you are still part of the top eight, but your interaction level may be lower than the first four friends.

6

Saturn on Snapchat

Saturn is the sixth position. It shows that you are in someone’s Best Friends list, but not among the closest positions. It can still be meaningful because only eight friends appear in the Solar System.

7

Uranus on Snapchat

Uranus is the seventh planet. This means you are close enough to appear in the top eight, but your activity is near the lower side of the ranking.

8

Neptune on Snapchat

Neptune is the eighth planet and the farthest from the Sun. On Snapchat, Neptune means you are in the eighth position in your friend’s Solar System. It is the last visible Best Friends position in the planet order.

Changes

Why Snapchat Planets Change

Snapchat Planets can change because your interaction with friends changes. If you start snapping and chatting more with one person, your position may move closer. If you stop interacting as much, your position may move farther away or disappear from the top eight.

Common reasons for changes include more Snaps, fewer chats, new active friends, broken streaks, changes in daily communication, or your friend interacting more with other people. This makes the ranking flexible instead of permanent.

Users should remember that Snapchat activity changes quickly. A friend can move from Mars to Venus or from Earth to Jupiter depending on recent app behavior.

Important Note

Snapchat Planets vs Real Friendship

One mistake many users make is treating Snapchat Planets like real-life friendship proof. This can create confusion, jealousy, or overthinking. Snapchat Planets only show app activity. They do not know your real emotions, private conversations outside Snapchat, family relationships, school friendships, or offline bonding.

Someone may be your best friend in real life but not appear as Mercury because you do not use Snapchat together often. Another person may rank higher simply because you send more Snaps or maintain a streak. So the feature should be seen as fun and informational, not emotional proof.

Benefits

Benefits of Snapchat Planets

Snapchat Planets can be fun for users who enjoy visual features and friendship rankings. It makes Snapchat+ feel more interactive and gives users a playful way to understand their activity with friends.

Some benefits include:

Snapchat Planets make the Best Friends list more visual and easier to understand. They give Snapchat+ users a fun reason to check Friendship Profiles. They also help users see which friends they interact with most on the app. For people who enjoy streaks, emojis, badges, and social features, the planet system adds another layer of interest.

Downsides

Possible Downsides of Snapchat Planets

Even though the feature is fun, it can also create pressure. Some users may feel upset if they are not the planet they expected. Others may compare themselves with someone else’s friends or overthink why their rank changed.

This is why Snapchat Planets should be used lightly. A planet position is not a relationship score. It is only a Snapchat activity signal. If users understand this, the feature becomes less stressful and more enjoyable.

Feature Missing

Why Snapchat Planets May Not Show

Sometimes users cannot see Snapchat Planets. This may happen because they do not have Snapchat+, the feature is not enabled, the badge is not available, the friend is not in the top eight, the app needs an update, or the account does not meet the feature conditions.

If the feature is missing, users can update Snapchat, check their Snapchat+ settings, restart the app, and confirm whether the Friendship Profile shows the correct badge. If it still does not appear, the feature may not be active for that account or region.

Privacy

Privacy and Visibility

Snapchat Planets are mostly designed as a private feature. When you check a friend’s Solar System, it shows your position in their ranking. It does not publicly display a full list of all their friends to everyone. However, users should still be careful because friendship ranking features can feel sensitive.

The best approach is to avoid using the feature to judge others. Snapchat is an app for communication and fun. Real friendship should not depend only on a planet badge.

Tips

Tips to Understand Snapchat Planets Better

To understand Snapchat Planets correctly, always remember the order from Mercury to Neptune. Mercury is the closest position, and Neptune is the farthest position. Also remember that the ranking belongs to the friend’s Solar System, not always yours.

If your planet changes, do not panic. It usually means Snapchat activity has changed. More interaction can improve your position, while less interaction can lower it. But the ranking should never become more important than real communication.

Final Verdict

Conclusion

Snapchat Planets are part of Snapchat’s Friend Solar System feature and help Snapchat+ users understand their position in a friend’s Best Friends list. The planets follow the order Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Mercury means the closest position, while Neptune means the eighth position.

The feature is useful because it turns friendship ranking into a simple visual system. However, users should not take it too seriously. Snapchat Planets are based on app interaction, not complete real-life friendship. They can change over time depending on Snaps, Chats, and activity patterns.

In the end, Snapchat Planets are best enjoyed as a fun feature. Use them to understand Snapchat activity, but do not let them create stress, jealousy, or confusion. Real friendship is bigger than any app ranking.
